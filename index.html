About

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical Engineering at Texas A&M University working in power and energy systems. My research focuses on resilient operation of electric power distribution systems, with emphasis on black-start restoration, maximal load delivery, microgrid coordination, distributed energy resource integration, and uncertainty-aware optimization under outage and contingency conditions.

My work combines mathematical optimization, physics-based distribution-system modeling, multi-phase unbalanced power-flow analysis, and large-scale simulation. I develop computational workflows using Julia, JuMP, PowerModelsDistribution.jl, PowerModelsONM.jl, OpenDSS, Python, and mixed-integer optimization solvers to support restoration decision-making for active distribution networks with inverter-based resources, photovoltaic generation, battery energy storage systems, diesel generation, controllable switches, and critical loads.

My current and future research direction is to develop uncertainty-aware and scalable restoration strategies for active distribution systems by integrating robust optimization, DER coordination, real-time decision-making algorithms, pre-event anticipatory scheduling, and post-event restoration and reconfiguration under weather-driven load and DER uncertainty.

Selected Research Projects

Uncertainty-Aware Black-Start Restoration in Active Distribution Networks

Develops restoration models for distribution systems after widespread outages, with explicit representation of DER availability, switching actions, topology control, voltage and thermal constraints, load pickup timing, and restoration sequence feasibility.

  • Models sequential energization of loads and network sections using mixed-integer optimization.
  • Represents grid-forming inverter capability, DER commitment, and feeder-level topology constraints.
  • Evaluates restoration performance under uncertain load behavior, cold load pickup, PV variability, DER unavailability, and permanent fault scenarios.
  • Uses OpenDSS-based feeder models, Julia/JuMP optimization, and PowerModelsDistribution/PowerModelsONM formulations.

Keywords: black-start restoration, distribution restoration, DER coordination, grid-forming inverter, topology control, MILP, uncertainty.

Two-Stage Robust Optimization for Maximal Load Delivery

Studies robust maximal load delivery in large-scale distribution systems and networked microgrids under load uncertainty. The work uses master-subproblem decomposition to select restoration and dispatch decisions and then tests feasibility against uncertain operating conditions.

  • Formulates a two-stage robust optimization structure for restoration feasibility under uncertain load levels.
  • Uses uncertainty sets for real and reactive load variation and subproblem-based feasibility checking.
  • Investigates feasibility-cut generation, worst-case load evaluation, and restoration decisions that remain feasible under uncertainty.
  • Targets large-scale distribution systems with DER, storage, PV, diesel generation, and switchable feeder sections.

Keywords: maximal load delivery, robust optimization, microgrid networking, feasibility cuts, uncertainty-aware operation.

Modified Iowa 240-Node Distribution System Studies

Builds and evaluates restoration studies on a modified Iowa 240-node unbalanced distribution system with multiple feeders, DERs, PV systems, BESS units, diesel generation, and switchable network sections.

  • Studies 24-step restoration behavior, DER dispatch, load pickup, and feeder reconfiguration.
  • Analyzes phase-level active and reactive power restoration, storage charging/discharging, state-of-charge trajectories, PV availability, and load restoration timing.
  • Uses the test feeder as a large-scale validation platform for black-start restoration, maximal load delivery, robust optimization, and scenario-based resilience assessment.

Keywords: Iowa 240-node feeder, unbalanced distribution system, OpenDSS, PV, BESS, diesel generation, feeder switching.

Power Loss Minimization in Active Distribution Systems

Investigates optimization-based power loss minimization in active distribution systems using PowerModelsDistribution and related distribution-system optimization workflows.

  • Studies loss-aware operation of active distribution networks with DER integration.
  • Develops computational studies using OpenDSS-derived network models and Julia-based optimization formulations.
  • Connects loss minimization studies to restoration-focused optimization for efficient post-outage operation.

Keywords: loss minimization, active distribution systems, PowerModelsDistribution, DER operation, distribution optimization.

Scenario-Based Resilience Evaluation

Designs and evaluates adverse operating scenarios for distribution-system resilience studies, including renewable reduction, DER outage, permanent fault conditions, and combined contingency cases.

  • Compares base-case operation with PV reduction, BESS or DER unavailability, non-restorable sections, and combined stress cases.
  • Evaluates restoration behavior using load restored, timing of restoration, phase-level power delivery, feasibility, switching actions, and DER dispatch behavior.
  • Supports quantitative comparison of restoration strategies under realistic operational uncertainty.

Keywords: resilience metrics, contingency analysis, PV reduction, DER outage, permanent fault, restoration feasibility.
