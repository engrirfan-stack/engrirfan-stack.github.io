About

I am a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University. My research focuses on optimization-driven resilient operation of electric power distribution systems, with emphasis on black-start restoration, maximal load delivery, microgrid coordination, distributed energy resources, and uncertainty-aware decision making under outage and contingency conditions.

My work integrates mathematical optimization, physics-based distribution-system modeling, and large-scale simulation to support automated restoration of active distribution networks with inverter-based resources, battery energy storage systems, photovoltaic generation, and controllable switching devices.

Research Interests

  • Maximum load delivery and microgrid networking

  • Uncertainty-aware and robust optimization
  • Topology reconfiguration, radiality, and switching constraints
  • Converter-based resources and grid-forming operation
  • OpenDSS, PowerModelsDistribution.jl, and PowerModelsONM.jl workflows

Selected Projects

Uncertainty-Aware Black-Start Restoration in Active Distribution Networks

Developed and evaluated optimization models for sequential restoration of distribution systems after widespread outages, considering DER availability, switching decisions, load pickup, voltage constraints, and restoration timing.

Keywords: black-start restoration, MILP, DER coordination, topology control, outage recovery

Two-Stage Robust Maximal Load Delivery for Networked Microgrids

Studied robust restoration and maximum load delivery using master-subproblem decomposition, uncertainty sets for load variation, and feasibility-cut generation to improve restoration feasibility under uncertain operating conditions.

Keywords: robust optimization, maximal load delivery, microgrids, feasibility cuts, uncertainty

Modified Iowa 240-Node Distribution System Studies

Conducted restoration and DER-dispatch studies on a modified unbalanced distribution system with multiple feeders, photovoltaic resources, battery energy storage systems, diesel generation, and switchable network sections.

Keywords: Iowa 240-node system, OpenDSS, unbalanced distribution systems, PV, BESS

Scenario-Based Resilience Evaluation

Designed contingency scenarios involving photovoltaic reduction, DER unavailability, permanent faults, and combined adverse conditions to evaluate restoration performance and operational resilience.

Keywords: resilience metrics, contingency analysis, DER outage, PV reduction, permanent fault
