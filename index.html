I am a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical Engineering at Texas A&M University, working on uncertainty-aware and resilient grid operation. My research develops optimization-driven and physics-based methods for restoring and operating active distribution systems under uncertain load, DER availability, renewable variability, and contingency conditions.

My work focuses on black-start restoration, maximal load delivery, topology reconfiguration, restoration sequencing, and DER coordination. I also build an automated restoration analysis framework that uses Python to preprocess system models, configure scenarios, run Julia/JuMP optimization with PowerModelsONM.jl and PowerModelsDistribution.jl, update post-restoration system states, and generate Excel summaries, DER dispatch plots, storage state-of-charge profiles, and PowerPlots network visualizations.

I am extending this research toward resilient and adaptive operation of data-center and critical power infrastructure, applying restoration, optimization, forecasting, automation, and physics-based simulation methods to converter-interfaced, high-density electric infrastructure.

Research Areas

  • Microgrid networking, PV/BESS/diesel coordination, and storage state-of-charge constrained restoration.
