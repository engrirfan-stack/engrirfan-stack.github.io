Research Profile

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical Engineering at Texas A&M University. My work focuses on resilient power distribution systems, especially restoration and reconfiguration after widespread outages. I formulate mixed-integer and robust optimization models that coordinate grid-forming resources, photovoltaic generation, battery energy storage systems, diesel generation, switching devices, and critical loads.

My computational work integrates OpenDSS, Julia, JuMP, PowerModelsDistribution.jl, PowerModelsONM.jl, Python, Gurobi, and HiGHS for physics-aware modeling, optimization, simulation, and post-processing of large-scale unbalanced distribution systems.

Current Research Themes

Black-start restoration

Optimization-based restoration of active distribution networks with grid-forming DERs, radial topology constraints, switching decisions, and sequential load pickup.

Robust maximal load delivery

Two-stage robust optimization for restoring maximum feasible load under uncertain real and reactive demand conditions in large-scale distribution systems.

DER and microgrid coordination

Coordinated operation of PV, BESS, diesel generation, and inverter-based resources for resilient post-outage distribution system operation.

Robust operation of data-center power infrastructure

Emerging research direction on optimization and resilience methods for data-center electric infrastructure, including reliable operation under load growth, DER variability, and contingency conditions.

Selected Projects

  • Uncertainty-aware black-start restoration: restoration models with DER availability, cold load pickup, voltage and thermal feasibility, topology control, and uncertain load behavior.
  • Two-stage robust MLD: master-subproblem restoration framework with uncertainty sets, feasibility evaluation, and robust load delivery decisions.
  • Modified Iowa 240-node studies: large-scale unbalanced feeder studies with PV, BESS, diesel generation, switchable sections, and 24-step restoration behavior.
  • Scenario-based resilience assessment: PV reduction, DER outage, permanent fault, and combined adverse operating scenarios for restoration-performance evaluation.
